		It has not any vertebral body. In the atlas we therefore describe 
		a smaller anterior arch and a larger posterior arch. Both arches have small 
		protuberances: the anterior and posterior tubercles. Lateral to the large vertebral 
		foramen of the atlas lie the lateral masses, each of which has a superior and an inferior 
		articular facet. On the inner side of the anterior arch is the articular facet for the dens, 
		fovea dentis. From the foramen of the transverse process, which is located in the processus transversus,
		a groove, the sulcus arteriae vertebralis, extends across the posterior arch for the reception of the  vertebral artery.
